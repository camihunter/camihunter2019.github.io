Repetition

Khan Academy

khanacademy.org

Khan Academy has done a great job using repetition on their homepage. All of their graphics are a form of collage using cut out images of people, rough sketched graphics, and cut out shapes of textures. As you scroll down the page, there are unique designs highlighting their site, yet they are all cohesive in their use of these elements.

Rule of Thirds

Cafe Zupas

cafezupas.com

The focal point on Cafe Zupas' homepage is an image with text overlay that fills most of the page. The image rotates between three graphics, but each of them use the rule of thirds to put their message at the top of the hierarchy. Your eye focuses first on the text because of the placement, then easily moves to explore the photograph behind the text. This also helps create a visually pleasing composition.

White Space and Clean Design

Adobe

adobe.com

Adobe's whole website utilizes white space and simple black text to highlight the bright images used on their site. Their site is full of beautiful, complicated, and colorful graphics that could be very noisy if placed too close together. The big chunks of white space proved balance, and the black text grounds the use of color.
